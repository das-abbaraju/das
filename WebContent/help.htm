Help - Red Flag Report

Red Flag Criteria

You have complete control over which questions you want to show up in your Red Flag Report, and what answers will cause a contractor to be flagged. You can edit this informations on the Red Flag Criteria Report. There are 2 sections - OSHA Questions, and other PQF questions. A flag will appear on your contractor list when the data entered by the contractor causes the Flag Test to be TRUE. For example:

  • If the question is: 'Do you perform background investigations and screening on your employees?'
  • You want this to trigger when they do NOT perform background tests
  • To achieve this, under the Flag Test column, select the Equal Sign (=) from the first drop down, and 'No' from the second. Remeber, the contractor is flagged if this critiria is True
  • You must also select the check box under the 'Include?' column for this question to be included in your Red Flag Report
  • This will cause a flag to appear next to the question if your criteria is true, i.e. if they answered the question with 'No' in this case
  • The Red Flag Report for that contractor will show an overall flagged status that is the most strict out of all the questions. If none of the criteria was flagged at all, then they will show a Green flag. If any questions are flagged with an Amber flag but no Red flags, then the status will be Amber. Any red flags will cause the contractor's overall flagged status to be Red.
  • Additionally, adding a contractor to a Waived or Forced Flag List will cause them to have a Green or Red flag, respectively

Waived/Forced Flag List

The Waived/Forced Flag List section gives your organization the opportunity to overide, either positively or negatively, the flagged status for a contractor. For example:

  • If they get flagged because of an answer to a specific question, and you still want them to be approved, you can override their red/amber flag by clicking the 'Add' button in the Waived Flag List row
  • They can not be on the list indefinitely. For this reason, you must select an Expiration Date of the form mm/dd/yy (ie 3/25/09). You can click on the calendar icon to select the date
  • Their Forced Flag Status, along with the expiration date, will then show in the Status column
  • To remove a contractor from either list, click on the 'Remove' Button
  • If a contractor passes your Red Flag Criteria, yet you would still like them to be flagged to prevent them from working at any of your facilities, you may add them to the Forced Flag List by selecting an expiration date, and clicking the 'Add' button in that row
  • Once the expiration date arrives, you do not have to do anything to removed the contractor from your Forced Flag or Waived Flag list. They will automatically be removed

Notes

The Notes section of the Red Flag Report is to help your company communicate internally about a specific contractor, especially if you have several different facilities.


  • Add a note by entering your text in the 'New Note' box, and clicking the 'Add Note' button
  • The note is then added to the running list of Notes. The person's name who wrote the note, and the date, are automatically logged as well.
  • Whenever a contractor is added to or removed from a Forced/Waived Flag list, it is also automatically recorded in the notes section
  • You can remove a note from the list by clicking the 'Delete' link. However, the note is still archived in the PICS system for future reference if needed
